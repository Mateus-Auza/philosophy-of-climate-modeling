# Climate Modeling and Epistemic Reliability

**Author:** Mateus Auza Cruz  
**Date:** 2026-05-15

## Overview

This repository contains an academic essay examining the epistemological foundations of climate modeling and its role in policymaking under conditions of uncertainty.

The essay argues that climate simulations remain epistemically reliable despite their inability to provide deterministic certainty. Drawing on the philosophy of science, it defends the view that **robustness across multiple models** and **adequacy-for-purpose** provide sufficient justification for using climate simulations in rational policy decisions.

## Research Question

How can climate simulations be considered reliable sources of scientific knowledge when they operate under significant uncertainty?

## Thesis

Although climate simulations cannot provide deterministic certainty, they remain epistemically reliable because robustness across multiple models and adequacy-for-purpose provide sufficient justification for rational policy decisions.

## Main Topics

- The epistemological challenges of climate modeling
- Uncertainty and complex systems
- Hume's problem of induction
- Popper's theory of falsifiability
- Ensemble modeling and robustness
- Model democracy and weighting
- Adequacy-for-purpose (Wendy S. Parker)
- Objections to climate modeling and philosophical responses
- Climate simulations as tools for policymaking
